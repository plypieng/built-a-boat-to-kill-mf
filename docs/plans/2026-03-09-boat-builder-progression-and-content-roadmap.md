# Boat Builder Progression And Content Roadmap

Date: 2026-03-09

## Overview

This document defines how the builder, propulsion families, station depth, and run content should expand over time.

The approved direction is:

- progression unlocks new machine identities, not flat dominance
- each new tier should open new archetypes and new crew stories
- old parts should remain relevant because they are simpler, cheaper, or better in specific roles
- progression should expand choice density slowly enough that the builder remains readable

## Progression Principles

- unlock `options`, not inevitability
- keep early parts viable as low-workload or low-risk tools
- let late parts create stronger specialization rather than just larger numbers
- ensure each propulsion family has a meaningful reason to exist
- tie progression to both economic reward and mastery reward

## Reward Sources

Recommended progression inputs:

- `gold`
  Broad unlock currency
- `salvage`
  Mechanical unlock currency for advanced modules and propulsion
- future optional mastery tokens:
  - rescue commendations
  - storm survey data
  - engine schematics

The first strong version only needs gold and salvage.

## Unlock Structure

Use five content tiers.

### Tier 0: Dockyard Basics

Purpose:

- let players launch weird boats quickly
- teach hull, cargo, salvage, and recovery fundamentals

Content:

- Core Keel
- Helm
- Pontoon Hull
- Deck Plate
- Cargo Pod
- Light Crane
- Ladder Rig
- Raft Paddles

Archetypes enabled:

- scrap raft
- tiny rescue skiff
- unstable cargo tray

### Tier 1: Coastal Work

Purpose:

- introduce first meaningful specialization
- improve safety and routeing
- offer first alternative propulsion identity

Content:

- Keel Hull
- Guard Rail
- Repair Bay
- Utility Bay
- Galley Paddles
- Fore Sail Rig

Archetypes enabled:

- stable labor boat
- simple sail runner
- basic repair-support hull

### Tier 2: Rough Water

Purpose:

- introduce storm specialization and heavier salvage
- make support layout matter more

Content:

- Reinforced Prow
- Outrigger Beam
- Brace Frame
- Heavy Winch
- Cutter Rig
- Steam Tug Drive

Archetypes enabled:

- storm-resistant cat-beam workboat
- proper cargo tug
- aggressive salvage platform

### Tier 3: Industrial Reach

Purpose:

- deepen machine identity and protection decisions
- introduce more demanding drives and better salvage tools

Content:

- Armored Housing
- Harpoon Crane
- Rescue Net
- Twin Screw Engine

Archetypes enabled:

- armored engine cutter
- high-risk salvage hunter
- safer rescue tug

### Tier 4: Advanced Yard

Purpose:

- unlock strong creative expression
- reward players who understand role coverage and layout tradeoffs

Content:

- Shock Bulkhead
- Catamaran Beam
- Auxiliary Kicker
- High-Pressure Boiler

Archetypes enabled:

- split-hull hybrid
- heavy industrial steam monster
- high-redundancy extraction craft

## Unlock Cost Philosophy

Use escalating cost, but keep early experimentation cheap.

Recommended shape:

- Tier 0 to Tier 1: cheap enough to unlock several toys quickly
- Tier 2: noticeable commitment
- Tier 3: specialization gate
- Tier 4: prestige and mastery gate

Avoid:

- huge dead grind walls
- requiring one strict optimal path to remain viable

## Propulsion Roadmap

### Early Game

- paddles dominate
- labor and stamina are part of the fantasy
- the crew learns that boat operation is physical

### Mid Game

- sail and steam split the tree
- sail rewards route reading and lightness
- steam rewards infrastructure and stable work platforms

### Late Game

- twin engine and hybrid packages appear
- machine management and redundancy become major build decisions

## Station Roadmap

The station system should deepen slowly.

### Early Game

- Helm
- Salvage
- basic paddle support

### Mid Game

- trim
- reefing
- venting
- repair zone play

### Late Game

- cooling
- engine isolation
- pressure management
- hybrid support tasks

Rule:

do not introduce more than one new persistent role category per major milestone.

## Content Roadmap By System

### Hull Content

Milestone order:

1. basic hull and deck pieces
2. reinforced forward plating
3. stabilizers and outriggers
4. split-hull support
5. shock and redundancy pieces

### Salvage Content

Milestone order:

1. Light Crane
2. Heavy Winch
3. Harpoon Crane
4. future specialist recovery tools if needed

### Recovery Content

Milestone order:

1. Ladder Rig
2. Guard Rail
3. Rescue Net
4. future side recovery rigs

### Support Content

Milestone order:

1. Repair Bay
2. Utility Bay
3. Brace Frame
4. Shock Bulkhead

## Biome And Hazard Synergy

Progression should matter because the world asks different questions.

### Open Ocean

- favors simple balanced boats

### Squall Lanes

- reward stable hulls, reefing discipline, and safety rails

### Salvage-Dense Routes

- reward strong brace coverage, heavy winch support, and repair access

### Long Extraction Routes

- reward efficient propulsion and redundancy

### Rescue-Heavy Seeds

- reward wide decks, recovery access, and low crew-loss risk

## Archetype Roadmap

The roadmap should preserve a healthy spread of archetypes.

### Work Barge

- low speed
- high cargo
- high survivability
- low panic

### Storm Cutter

- high speed
- sharp extraction control
- low forgiveness

### Rescue Tug

- high crew safety
- strong recovery support
- modest haul

### Sail Runner

- route-smart
- low constant labor
- fragile under bad weather handling

### Hybrid Specialist

- highest creative ceiling
- highest layout and workload complexity

## Economy Philosophy

- early rewards should buy breadth
- mid-game rewards should buy identity
- late-game rewards should buy expression and refinement

The player should never feel that they are only saving for one obviously mandatory engine.

## Decision Log

- Decision: progression unlocks new machine identities
  Alternative: progression as flat stat ladder
  Resolution: rejected because it kills build creativity
- Decision: preserve old parts as viable options
  Alternative: every new tier obsoletes the previous one
  Resolution: rejected because it narrows the sandbox
- Decision: split mid-game propulsion into sail and steam
  Alternative: jump directly from paddles to engines
  Resolution: rejected because it leaves too little variety in the middle of the game

## Success Criteria

This roadmap is successful when:

- players can pursue visibly different boat fantasies across progression
- there is no single obviously mandatory propulsion path
- the builder becomes richer without becoming unreadable
- old parts remain situationally valuable
- late-game creativity comes from composition, not raw power inflation
